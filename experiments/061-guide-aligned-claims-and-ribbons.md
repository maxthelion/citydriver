# 061 Guide-Aligned Claims And Residual Ribbons

This experiment takes the `060` structure:

- boundary-attached park
- one strongest remaining commercial edge
- park-side terraces
- residual cross streets and ribbons

and changes one thing upstream:

- build an early cross-street guide lattice for the selected sector
- snap the park frontage span to that lattice
- snap the post-park commercial frontage span to that same lattice
- then fill the residual using that same phase

So the intended structure is:

- guide lines first
- claims sized against the guide rhythm
- residual street fill inherits the exact same rhythm

Questions:

- Does this make the claims and the residual fill read like one coherent system?
- Do the park and commercial edges feel cleaner when their widths come from guide intervals?
- Is the early guide lattice a better shared planning artifact than sizing claims independently and reconciling later?
